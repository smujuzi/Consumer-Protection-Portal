  • Electronic Transactions Act
    A. AGREEMENT OF SCALE

    1. Should a supplier providing goods or services through electronic transactions provide for agreements to be executed between the supplier and buyer?

    All suppliers providing goods or services through electronic transactions should provide for agreements with details of the terms and conditions that will apply to the transaction.

    2. Can I access the terms and conditions and in what form should they be?

    Yes, a customer should be able to access the terms and conditions. The terms and conditions should be accessed, stored and reproduced electronically by the consumer.

    B. SECURITY AND PAYMENT INFORMATION AND PRIVACY

    3. Is my payment information supplied online to a supplier of goods and services secure?

    Suppliers are required to ensure that they use secure payment systems and have security procedures in place to safeguard the payment information you supply them when buying goods or services electronically. It is therefore advisable that you review the supplier’s website to establish whether they have security measures and procedures in place, for example websites which have a security lock icon displayed at the beginning of their URL demonstrates that the website is “Secure” or “Verified”; and it should also possess a URL which starts with “https” instead of “http” which means the site is secured using an SSL Certificate (the s stands for secure). SSL Certificates secure all of your data as it is passed from your browser to the website's server. To some extent, this provides some comfort that the website is fairly secure. You can also use safe payment platforms like paypal to make payments to the suppliers as this to a larger extent reduces the likelihood of your payment information being compromised and accessed by unauthorized persons or even abused. Suppliers should also ensure that they have privacy policies in place and adhere to them.

    4. Suppliers tend to send unsolicited messages after I have used their website. How can I stop these messages from coming through to my email address?

    Whereas it is not illegal for a supplier to send unsolicited messages to a buyer, suppliers are required to give buyers an option to cancel subscription to a mailing list and this should be at no cost to the buyer.

    C. CANCELLATION AND REFUND OF PAYMENT FOR GOODS OR SERVICES

    5. Can I cancel an electronic transaction for the supply of goods or services?

    Yes, within 7 (seven) days after the date of receipt of the goods or services; or {Incomplete}

    6. Do I get a full refund after cancellation of an electronic transaction for the supply of goods or services?

    Yes, you are entitled to a refund, less the costs for returning the goods (these costs could be shipping/courier costs)

    7. Within what period must a supplier of goods or services refund a payment where the customer cancels an electronic transaction?

    The supplier must refund the money paid for the goods or services within 30 (thirty) days from the date of cancellation.

    8. After placing an order for goods or services, within what period shall I receive the goods or services?

    The supplier must execute the order within 30 (thirty) days unless otherwise agreed by the supplier and buyer.

    9. Can I cancel the order if the supplier fails to execute the order?

    Yes, you may cancel the order within 30 (thirty) days or other period agreed to by the supplier and buyer. However, the buyer must give the supplier 7 (seven) days written notice prior to cancellation.

    10. If the supplier fails to supply the goods due to in availability of the goods or services, am I entitled to a refund?

    The supplier is required to inform you of the unavailability of the goods or services before the expiry of the delivery period (30 (thirty) days or any other time agreed to by the supplier and buyer) and the buyer is entitled to a refund within 30 (thirty) days.

  • IT Certification

    1. What is the purpose of certifying a provider of IT goods and services?

    The purpose of the Regulations is to provide a legal framework for regulating providers of Information Technology products and services to ensure consumer protection.

    2. Should I deal with a provider that is not certified by NITA-U?

    We advise that you only deal with providers that are certified by NITA-U as a person is prohibited from providing IT goods and services without being certified by NITA-U.

    3. What are the benefits of dealing with a certified provider of IT goods and services?

    It is reassurance that the provider is legally conducting the business of providing IT goods and services and is qualified to provide the goods and services; and confirms that the provider meets the requirements and standards under the Regulations.

    4. How do I know that I am dealing with a certified provider of IT goods and services?

    You may request the provider to produce a certificate issued by the National Information Technology Authority, Uganda confirming that they are certified. You may also visit the NITA-U website and check the list of published certified providers.

    5. How can I verify the authenticity of the certificate of a provider of IT goods and services?

    You may visit the IT Certification web portal www.itco.go.ug accessible through the NITA-U website and check the list of published certified providers. Further, if you are evaluating a provider for a bid, you may verify the authenticity of the Certification certificate by scanning the QR Code on the certificate using a QR Code scanner.

    6. What is the validity of a certificate issued by the Authority to a provider of IT goods and services?

    A certificate shall be valid for 1 (one) year from the date of issue.

    7. In case a dispute arises between a consumer and a provider of IT goods and services, what should the consumer do.

    In case you have a contract with the provider, you may invoke the dispute resolution mechanism that has been provided for. Where the contract provides for an alternative dispute resolution mechanism like mediation or arbitration, the Parties may select a mediator or arbitrator or may refer the matter to NITA-U for mediation or arbitration. In case the Parties agree that where arbitration fails, they may refer the matter to the courts of law, then a party may take the matter to the courts.

    8. Do foreign companies have to be certified by NITA-U to carry out business in Uganda?

    Yes, the requirement for IT Certification also applies to foreign companies or individuals that wish to provide goods or services in Uganda. Such provider will make an application for Certification from NITA-U and if they meet the requirements under the IT Certification Regulations, they will be granted a Certificate.

    9. Would a certification or accreditation issued by an authorized foreign organization to a foreign company that is carrying out business in Uganda or is interested in conducting business in Uganda satisfy NITA-U’s requirement for a provider to be certified in order to conduct business in Uganda?

    No. A certification or accreditation issued by an authorized foreign organization to a foreign company that is carrying out business in Uganda or is interested in conducting business in Uganda would not satisfy NITA-U’s requirements for certification. A foreign company would still be required to apply for certification by NITA-U.

    10. When can a provider’s certification be suspended or cancelled?

    If the provider acts in contravention of the NITA-U Act or NITA-U (Certification of Providers of Information Technology Products or Services) (Amendment) Regulations 2018 or where the provider’s capacity to provide the IT products or services has diminished in a manner that affects the certification.

  • Quality Assurance
    Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) for E-Commerce Consumers

    1. What are cookies?

    Cookies are pieces of information about your activity on a website. They store information that can help the website owner customise your user experience based on your preferences. Cookies may be used to recommend products to customers based on the items they frequently search for or buy.

    2. Is it safe to allow/accept cookies?

    Yes, it is safe to allow/accept cookies. However, some websites may allow third parties to use their cookies to deliver ads and other content through their website. This may indirectly give information about your activities to third parties who then use it to send targeted ads to you. You can enable cookies and also prevent being tracked by third parties by having strict privacy restriction in your browser. Review your browser’s privacy features.

    3. What is encryption and how is it helpful to a customer when shopping online?

    Encryption is a mechanism used to hide information. While transferring information between a website and a customer, encryption is used to hide the customer and vendor’s information to keep it private. A customer’s credit/debit card data, personal data, transaction history and other information are kept private using encryption.

    4. How can a customer know/check that an e-commerce platform/website is using encryption?

    In the address bar of every browser, before the website address, there is a padlock icon that shows that a website is encrypted. A padlock is the universal symbol for encryption. An open padlock means there is no encryption and the icon may also have a cancelled symbol around it.

    5. What is a chatbot/bot and how do I know if my complaint is received by the right person?

    A chatbot is a software tool, an innovative way to quickly help customers find what they need without directly contacting a human. However, some hackers can compromise a website and use pop-up windows to trick a customer into a chat conversation where they will ask the customer to verify their details by entering them in a text field. Be cautious of such attempts.

    6. What is a strong password?

    A strong password is one that cannot be easily guessed from information known about a customer. A strong password should have 8 or more characters and contain alphanumeric symbols such as (! ? # @ + & $ - )

    7. What responsibilities do customers of e-commerce platforms have to ensure their online payments are secure?

    A customer should ensure they are using the vendor’s Mobile app or website and no other means to make online payments. When making payments using a vendor’s website you need to be keen to verify whether the website URL/address is correct and secured, evidenced by the security padlock icon against their URL. You need to also make sure you are using an updated browser and an updated computer operating system.

    8. What internet risks do I face while using e-commerce platforms?

    There are a number of threats that may pose a risk to using e-commerce platforms and they present themselves in various forms. However, they can all be neutralized. Some of these threats are hackers, scammers, fraudsters, viruses and ransomware to mention a few. Phishing is the most common method used by these actors to attack internet users.

    9. What is phishing?

    Phishing is an attempt to fraudulently obtain sensitive information such as usernames and passwords or credit/debit card data by disguising as a trustworthy entity or impersonating another entity known to the targeted victim. This is often done through email communication.

    10. Can my credit card data be stolen while I am making an online transaction?

    It may be possible due to some unfortunate cases. If a customer visited a website that looks exactly like the vendor’s website they intended to go to without checking the website address to verify if it’s correct then it’s likely that the customer is going to initiate a transaction and enter his/her credit/debit card data only to be stolen by malicious people/groups. Another scenario is when a customer receives an email (also called a phishing email) of a sale promotion or discount asking him/her to click a link to immediately redeem the discount/offer. The link opens a page or redirects the customer to a page that looks exactly like the vendor’s website where the products are to be bought and prompts the customer to enter their credit/debit card data.

    11. What should I do after falling victim of online fraud?

    Step 1: Report the incident to the National Computer Emergency Response Team – CERT and give a full description of how it happened. Attach some screenshots for more precise details. Step 2: Notify your bank of the incident and ask them to block your account. Step 3: Reset/Change the password which you used on the e-commerce platform from which you were impersonated as well as for your email account through which you received the phishing email (In case of phishing). Step 4: If your incident started with a phishing/fraudulent email, go to your email inbox and mark the email you received as Spam. If you were using a business email (yourname@company.com) then you need to consider switching to a personal email account that you can use for all personal online activity.
